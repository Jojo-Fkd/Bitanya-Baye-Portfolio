Beginnings . . .

This is Chil@d

This conceptual work started at first as Bitanya felt inspired by the nostalgic experience he had as a child with play
As time progressed with experiment he began to start and recognize his intrinsic; emotions, memories, thoughts and Interactions with the extrinsic.
The conceptual process continued in trying to scrutinize how the outer world has shaped his identity in general.
State of the inner child
The conceptual practice has helped to recognize emotional intelligence, social conditioning and the state of the inner child. As art has power to evoke the truth, it has helped ME Bitanya Baye to evoke what is from within, recognize and acknowledge all aspects of one's self (self discovery).
Then came “CHIL@DA”, the persona that drives the works I apply to study myself, ‘what is my value?’, ‘what do my traits resemble?’, what I believe I am made out to be...

I was strongly influenced by Western media as a teenager, immersing myself in cinema and music, which greatly improved my English proficiency. This made me somewhat of an outcast in high school, as I spent more time with my sketchbook and Hollywood media. This contributed to the development of a specific character with a distinct attitude and mindset, which continued to manifest itself throughout my adolescence. As I began to pursue my artistic passions, this character started to materialize through my creative works.

Cil@d? Please elaborate

You see I have always found myself, as a kid immersed in different streams of artistic expressions and creativity outlets, but now as a young adult I observed I have to be clear with what I have to do and what I want.

So at art school I have began to develop the ever existing persona chil@d...

At first it started with figuring out what pseudonym would fit this artistic persona of mine, while I was working with an academic work, I felt inspired by the endemic animal of Ethiopia, since some of my relatives are from there, and came to visit from Semien Mountain and tell me different stories about the Gelada baboon.
Now that it is clear to me this persona is very artistic and expressive in a sense of visual arts, it has become a stream of my ambitions I have been working on for the past 6 to 7 years of my life.
